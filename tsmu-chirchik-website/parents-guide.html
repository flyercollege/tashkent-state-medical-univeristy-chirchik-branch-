Overview

Key information parents usually need first.

These points cover the practical questions families ask before enrollment and during the first academic year.

Academic Programs

Students may study General Medicine, Pharmacy, Medical Pediatrics or Psychology depending on admission results and university policy.

Tuition and Contracts

Official tuition, contracts and payment schedules must be confirmed through university offices rather than agents or informal groups.

Hostel Guidance

Accommodation requests are subject to capacity. Early communication with the hostel and admissions teams helps families plan better.

Safety and Welfare

Campus rules, hostel procedures, emergency routing and student welfare support help students adapt safely and responsibly.

Clinical Practice

Hospital and clinic practice is organized according to faculty, year of study, clinical discipline and university scheduling.

International Support

International families receive routing for admission letters, visa guidance, arrival preparation, hostel requests and orientation.

Official Communication

Parents should use the correct office for admissions, finance, hostel, records and student affairs to avoid confusion.

Student Independence

How Parents Help

Useful support families can provide.

  • Encourage consistent attendance and responsible study habits.
  • Use official university communication for important questions.
  • Help students prepare documents and travel plans on time.
  • Discuss finances, living costs and expectations early.
  • Support adaptation without replacing the student in routine academic tasks.
Student Responsibility

What students should manage directly.

  • Class attendance, preparation and academic deadlines.
  • Respecting hostel, campus and clinical practice rules.
  • Checking official university announcements and instructions.
  • Communicating promptly with faculty and university offices.
  • Maintaining professionalism during practical and clinical training.
International Families

Support for parents whose student is moving to Uzbekistan.

International families usually need clearer coordination around visa processes, arrival timing, accommodation, orientation and early adaptation. TSMU Chirchik Branch routes these questions through Admissions, the International Department and the Hostel Office.

FAQ

Parent questions.

These are the most common questions families ask before enrollment and during the first months of study.

Can parents confirm admission directly with the university?

Yes. Parents should use official university channels, especially the Admissions Office and Contact page, to confirm admission-related information.

Is hostel accommodation guaranteed?

No. Hostel placement depends on capacity and university policy, so early application and clear communication are important.

Who supports international students after arrival?

The International Department, Hostel Office, Student Affairs and faculty-level support help students during arrival and adaptation.

Can parents ask about tuition or contracts?

Yes, but parents should verify payment and contract information only through official university finance routing.

How do students receive clinical practice?

Clinical practice is organized through approved partner hospitals and clinics according to the student's faculty, academic year and rotation plan.
